Luke 19:28-48

Jesus Comes to Jerusalem as King

28 After Jesus had said this, he went on ahead, going up to Jerusalem.29 As he approached Bethphage and Bethany at the hill called the Mount of Olives, he sent two of his disciples, saying to them,30 “Go to the village ahead of you, and as you enter it, you will find a colt tied there, which no one has ever ridden. Untie it and bring it here.31 If anyone asks you, ‘Why are you untying it?’ say, ‘The Lord needs it.’”

32 Those who were sent ahead went and found it just as he had told them.33 As they were untying the colt, its owners asked them, “Why are you untying the colt?”

34 They replied, “The Lord needs it.”

35 They brought it to Jesus, threw their cloaks on the colt and put Jesus on it.36 As he went along, people spread their cloaks on the road.

37 When he came near the place where the road goes down the Mount of Olives, the whole crowd of disciples began joyfully to praise God in loud voices for all the miracles they had seen:

“Peace in heaven and glory in the highest!”

Summary

A prophecy given 500 years earlier is fulfilled before these young leaders’ eyes. A king riding on a donkey will approach Jerusalem! (Zechariah 9:9). People were praising God as Jesus descended down the Mount of Olives on the road into the city of Jerusalem. The Pharisees understood the Messianic implications of the situation and told Jesus to tell His disciples to be quiet. But Jesus knew the importance that this testimony played in the Father’s plan. As Jesus approaches the city, He begins to weep. Jesus was grieved because He knew most of the people did not realize who He was or the significance of this event. He also knew that one day judgement would come on the city because of their unbelief; Jerusalem would be destroyed by the Roman army. Once again, we see Jesus’ passion displayed as he drives out the money changers who had returned to their old practices, taking advantage of people coming to worship God. Jesus not only showed passion for the Father’s reputation, but compassion for the poor and the foreigner.
