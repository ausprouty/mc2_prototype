Period 5: Leadership Multiplication

around Late 28 AD – Spring 30 AD


Multiplication of the movement: prayerfully selecting and developing new leaders, sending proven multipliers to continue as movement building leaders


This period lasts for about 15 months and begins with the appointing of the Twelve as Apostles. The Apostles become “leaders in training” within the larger group of ministry workers. There is still a larger group of followers and seekers (Luke 6:12-19). The Twelve are the fruit of Jesus’ deliberate selection, appointment, training and personal investment. This is further demonstrated after Pentecost through the Kingdom expansion captured in the Book of Acts.


Later in this period, Jesus sent out 70 others (Luke 10:1). Even though they were not given the same formal leadership status within the Gospel record as the Twelve, they too were commissioned as “apostles” (i.e. sent ones). We see the built-in multiplication of leaders as this new group was commanded to pray for the next generation of workers that would be raised up through their work (Luke 10:2). No doubt many of these un-named 70 missionaries were present in the early church, among the 120 in the upper room and included among the 500 people Jesus appeared to after His resurrection (1 Corinthians 15:6).
