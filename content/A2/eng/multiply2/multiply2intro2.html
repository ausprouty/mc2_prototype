Period 2: Ministry Foundations: around Spring 26 AD - Late 27 AD
Establishing a relational God-centered foundation for making disciples
This period starts in mid-26 AD when Jesus is about the age of 30 and lasts for 18-21 months. Jesus’ public ministry is initiated with His baptism then steadily grows through the initial contacts He made (John 1:35-44), finding His first followers and crowd that gathers to learn more about Him (John 4:1-4). It ends with His first rejection at His home village of Nazareth (Luke 4:16-31).
This period is characterized by ministry foundations being laid. Jesus is intentional in creating a relational environment where He gathers people as followers of the new way. Jesus’ ministry starts from nothing then grows over time until it is larger than the size of John the Baptist’s ministry (John 4:1-3; Matthew 4:12; Luke 3:19-20).
