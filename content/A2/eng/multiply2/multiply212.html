Summary

John moved his ministry north of Judea to continue baptizing people on the edge of the regions of the Decapolis and Samaria. Meanwhile, Jesus continued his ministry in Judean countryside while John continued his ministry of preparing the way for people to come to Jesus. As John’s disciples ask why Jesus’ ministry was growing bigger than John’s, John explains his perspective: he knew who Jesus was and who he was. He knew that the source of real joy was being near Jesus and listening to His voice. John knew that after he heard Jesus speak he could only obey. John modeled dying to self and being obedient to the Father’s will.
