6.

Prayer

Prayer saturated every aspect of Christ’s life and ministry. His ministry began in prayer and ended in prayer. Shortly after His baptism He was led by the Spirit into the wilderness, where He launched His ministry with 40 days of fasting and prayer.


Over 45 sections of Scripture covering 30 events record how Jesus would often slip away to pray.

+ While or just after praying...

  • The Spirit came upon Jesus, much like the disciples were commanded to wait for the gift promised. Like Jesus, they received the Spirit when “they all joined together constantly in prayer.”
  • Jesus changed the course of His ministry, even though a crowd was waiting for Him.
  • Jesus walked on water and challenged Peter to get out of the boat and come — causing great fear and ultimately worship as His disciples watched and experienced the supernatural.
  • A woman caught in adultery was brought to Jesus, and He exhibited God-ordained wisdom and compassion. Even Jesus’ critics were silenced, as one by one they left their condemning attitudes behind and walked away.
  • Jesus was transfigured and “the appearance of His faced changed” while with Peter, James and John on a mountain.
  • Jesus chose His twelve leaders.
  • Jesus received strength to face the horror of the cross.
  • Jesus also withdrew to a mountain to pray when the crowds tried to make Him king by force. His last words were even words of prayer, through which a centurion came to believe in Him.

 


For Jesus, prayer was a source of strength, a time to learn His Father’s heart, and a time to listen to His Father’s desires.

The Twelve Apostles, when pressured by a growing ministry, appointed spirit-filled men who would carry the burden of increasing responsibilities so that the Twelve could give their “attention to prayer and the ministry of the Word” (Acts 6:4).

The New Testament church, desiring to walk as Jesus walked, devoted itself to prayer as one of its foundational priorities.

+ Four Strategies for Raising Prayer

  • Daily 1-on-1 Prayer can raise up a crazy amount of prayer for your community.  It is very easy to set up.
  • Prayer Hotline takes a little more time to set up, but is invaluable for transforming critical events.
  • Bartimaeus Prayers have a special place for people who are far from God.
  • Prayer Gatherings build vision and faith.
