Period 2: Ministry Foundations: around Spring 26 AD - Late 27 AD
Establishing a relational God-centered foundation for making disciples

+ The Pattern from Christ

As Christ begins His ministry, He recognizes that it is not into a vacuum in which He begins to minister. He knows He ministers within a certain context and culture ... full of His Father’s agenda and His Father’s preparation.  

In Genesis after the Fall, God begins His grand meta-narrative to redeem man and restore His Kingdom rule on earth. God’s agenda was, through Israel, to offer redemption and restoration to fallen man. After 400 years of silence, it was into this Jewish context ... following on the heels of John the Baptist, that Jesus enters. John had “prepared the way” and now Jesus begins His ministry, submitting to His Father’s timing and His Father’s agenda. 

During His first year and a half of ministry, Jesus spends His time developing a close relationship with a smaller group of people rather than trying to reach the masses. He stays in the wilderness near the Jordan River and the smaller towns of Galilee. His priority is to spend time with His initial followers. 

During this phase, we see Christ ministering to a wide variety of people. The few miracles He performs are focused on revealing His identity to the people following Him. This period officially begins at Jesus’ baptism and continues until His rejection at Nazareth. During this time, Christ begins to prepare those He is with for the ministry ahead.  

The Ministry Foundations Phase is a time for leaders to build good relationships in preparation for the expanded ministry ahead. It is a time when we use our resources and energy to build an atmosphere of love, trust and acceptance as a foundation for future ministry.  

Jesus’ two main objectives during the Ministry Foundations Phase:

1. Create a relational, God-centred ENVIRONMENT for producing disciples through six foundational priorities:

  • Holy Spirit dependence
  • Prayer
  • Obedience to a Kingdom Agenda
  • Word of God
  • Exalting the Father
  • Relationships that are intentional

2. Begin to identify, THROUGH RELATIONSHIPS, a team of like-minded individuals committed to being involved in ministry
