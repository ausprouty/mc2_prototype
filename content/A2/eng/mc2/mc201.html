Goal: (10 minutes)

MC2 is designed to help your team follow the model that Jesus used for establishing multiplying spiritual communities where a majority of the spiritual community are regularly:

  1. Praying for the salvation of people in their social circles
  2. Deepening relationships by moving from casual to meaningful to spiritual conversations.
  3. Helping to meet the practical needs people in their social circles and wider community
  4. Sharing what God is doing in their life
  5. Praying with both Christians and non-Christians
  6. Reading the Bible with seekers
  7. Inviting groups of seekers to discover for themselves what it means to follow Jesus
  8. Helping to start new faith communities

We will be learning together through:

  • group learning experiences and
  • peer coaching

With frequent evaluations and mid-source corrections

Celebrating what God does in and through people

Over the next 12 to 24 months

 

It is going to be a lot of work.  There are going to be great joys and great sorrows along the way.  Our hope is that you will draw closer to God and people during this time. That He will transform your character and increase your skill as you depend on him.

Many people start the journey.  Those who continue and see it through have a firm conviction of why they started and what they want to see accomplished.

When Jesus' disciples asked him to teach them to pray he said: "This then is how you should pray: 'Our Father in heaven, hallowed be your name, your kingdom come, your will be done, on earth as it is in heaven."

What would it be like if all the people in your community God's will to be done on earth as it is in heaven?

Have participants get into groups of 3-4 people, to introduce themselves and discuss the following.  They will return to the same groups for prayer at the end of this session.

What is your vision? (15 minutes)

Get into groups of 3-4 people, introduce yourselves and discuss the following:

What would it look like if all the people in your community wanted God's will to be done on earth as it is in heaven?

  • What would they be talking about?
  • How would they dress?
  • What would they spend their money on?
  • What problems would disappear?
  • What would people start doing? Stop doing?
  • How would older people feel about the community?
  • What would happen to crime?
  • How would this impact hospitals?

The Gap (5 minutes)

+ Nehemiah’s Prayer

1:1 The words of Nehemiah son of Hakaliah:

In the month of Kislev in the twentieth year, while I was in the citadel of Susa,2 Hanani, one of my brothers, came from Judah with some other men, and I questioned them about the Jewish remnant that had survived the exile, and also about Jerusalem.

3 They said to me, “Those who survived the exile and are back in the province are in great trouble and disgrace. The wall of Jerusalem is broken down, and its gates have been burned with fire.”

4 When I heard these things, I sat down and wept. For some days I mourned and fasted and prayed before the God of heaven.5 Then I said:

“Lord, the God of heaven, the great and awesome God, who keeps his covenant of love with those who love him and keep his commandments,6 let your ear be attentive and your eyes open to hear the prayer your servant is praying before you day and night for your servants, the people of Israel. I confess the sins we Israelites, including myself and my father’s family, have committed against you.7 We have acted very wickedly toward you. We have not obeyed the commands, decrees and laws you gave your servant Moses.

8 “Remember the instruction you gave your servant Moses, saying, ‘If you are unfaithful, I will scatter you among the nations,9 but if you return to me and obey my commands, then even if your exiled people are at the farthest horizon, I will gather them from there and bring them to the place I have chosen as a dwelling for my Name.’

10 “They are your servants and your people, whom you redeemed by your great strength and your mighty hand.11 Lord, let your ear be attentive to the prayer of this your servant and to the prayer of your servants who delight in revering your name. Give your servant success today by granting him favor in the presence of this man.”

I was cupbearer to the king.
