7.1.

Scenarios

Scenario 1: Witnessing without holy living

Someone in your coaching circle is leading 2 Multiply Groups.  One group member is very active in ministry and always reports about talking with others about Jesus.  However, he  still is practicing bad habits.  His lifestyle includes drinking and smoking, sometimes they even get drunk.  Others in the Multiply Group have been discouraged by his actions and have begun to occasionally join him drinking.  Which scriptures can you use to coach in this situation?

Scenario 2: Not obeying what they were taught

A member of your coaching circle has been leading a Multiply Group for over one year.  Even though they were very active in prayer, care, share when they started, now things have cooled down.  Over the past three months, members do not do their “I will” statements anymore.  Some members have stopped coming.  Which scriptures can you use to coach in this situation?

Scenario 3: Come to Christ but not willing to work

A member of your coaching circle is leading a young house church made up of students and young professionals.  One student is in financial need and is often getting money from other members.  He does not get a job because he says he is too busy with his school work.  One day, he approaches the church about helping with his tuition bill.  Which scriptures can you use to coach in this situation?

Scenario 4: Muslim with several wives, wants to be a church leader

A member of your coaching circle has a muslim background believer in their group.  He came to Christ 8 months ago and his life has been radically changed.  He is a faithful witness to other muslims and has led many to Christ.  In fact, he is leading 2 Multiply Groups of his own and has shown leadership potential.  However, he has two wives, both of whom have also accepted Christ.  Which scriptures can you use to coach in this situation?
