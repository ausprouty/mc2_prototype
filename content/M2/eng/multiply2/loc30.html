Summary

Jesus was often misunderstood. John the Baptist had been sitting in prison and was struggling with doubts about Jesus being the one to bring in the Kingdom of God. So John sent a few of his remaining disciples to ask Jesus about it. Jesus sends the men back with an Old Testament scripture from Isaiah (which is the same one He used in Nazareth) to let John know He was doing the works of the Messiah. Jesus then endorses the leadership of John to His disciples. He contrasted this with the evil things people said about John and about Himself. Even after watching Jesus for over two years, religious leaders accused Jesus of being demon possessed, a glutton and a drunkard. We learn that being misunderstood is part of leadership.
